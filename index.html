The flag bearers of bengal's legacy

Far away from Bengal a dream for Bengalis saw the light of the day in 1958 when the erstwhile Chief minister of West Bengal, Dr. Bidhan Chandra Roy laid the foundation of Bengal Association - The flag bearers of Bengal's rich legacy, with an objective to connect Bengalis outside Bengal... to be connected to its socio-cultural roots.

He also visualized a dream for a widespread awareness and love for Bangla outside Bengal. A dream with such a huge motive can not fade away with one person.. the legacy continued and still continues.

Eminent personalities like Shri Pranab Mukherjee, Dr. Triguna Sen, Prof. Debiprasad Chattopadhyay, Prof. Humayun Kabir, Shri Somnath, Chatterjee and many more added to this noble thought.

our future aspirations

In the years ahead, the Association, in line with the vision of its founder, its leaders and initiatives of its affiliates, members, office bearers and well-wishers will always put in its best offerts for mightier accolades. With greatness of the land we belong to and the strength of our Motherland, each one of us feel the responsibility and the privilege of being the flag bearers of Bengal's legacy.

A dream of the Association is to create its own museum focusing around Bangla, through this museum it 's dream is to showcase to the world what Bengal comprises of. We want to organise small exhibitions of our Rural Art and which would facilitate the artisans of Bengal to get to a bigger audience. We want to help our kids who are otherwise studying in English medium schools to read and know the literary treasure of Bengal. We want to help our senior citizens in every possible way to connect with new smart world.

Possibilities are numerous and we all are dreaming as The Former President of India Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam stated.

"You have to dream before your dreams can come true",

Bengal Association has dreamt and is now working on realizing it. No dreams like this can be achieved with a singular effort but together we definitely can.

Bengal is one of the most culturally precious states of our country, and all of us at Bengal Association, Delhi are continously propagating the rich culture and heritage of Bengal in Delhi and NCR from the last seven decades. We are constantly moving ahead with a single goal in mind to build a Bengal outside Bengal by spreading its rich culture, tradition and most importantly the Bengali language.

I request you all to join hands with us in this pious initiative and resolve to continue the triumph of Bengali culture.

Shubhaya bhavatu.

Tapan Sengupte, General Secretary, Bengal Association - Delhi
